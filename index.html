To What Extent Do Fairness Judgment and Reward-Risk Predictive Error Learning Share Neural Substrates?

— A Narrative Synthesis

MSc Neuroscience · Narrative Synthesis

Examining whether the brain regions responsible for fairness judgements and reward prediction error signalling form a shared, domain-general architecture or operate as distinct neural modules.

🧠 fMRI & Lesion Evidence 📊 Computational Modelling 🔬 AI · VS · vmPFC · dACC
Abstract

This narrative synthesis examines whether fairness processing in the human brain relies on a domain-general prediction error (PE) architecture or a specialised neural module. Drawing on neuroimaging, computational modelling, and lesion studies, the synthesis evaluates the roles of the anterior insula (AI), ventral striatum (VS), ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC), and dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (dACC) across fairness and non-social reward learning paradigms. Converging evidence positions the VS as the central locus of signed RPE computation that generalises across social and non-social domains, while AI encodes unsigned salience of expectation violations. The vmPFC transforms PE signals into socially-modulated value representations, and dACC monitors conflict downstream of PE computation. Collectively, these findings support a partial-overlap model in which fairness emerges from the interaction of expectation formation, violation detection, PE computation, and value integration within domain-general reinforcement learning circuitry — rather than a dedicated fairness module.

Methodology

Narrative Synthesis Approach

A systematic narrative synthesis integrating neuroimaging, computational modelling, lesion, and electrophysiological evidence across fairness and reward PE paradigms.

🔍

Search Strategy

Two parallel searches targeting fairness-related neuroimaging and reward/risk PE learning literature, screened against inclusion criteria for human fMRI reporting region-specific activation.

📐

Synthesis Framework

Narrative synthesis with a partial-overlap model as the organising framework. Regions evaluated across four functional roles: expectation formation, violation detection, PE computation, and value integration.

⚠️

Caveats Addressed

Reverse inference risk (Ruff & Fehr, 2014), activation ≠ causation, fMRI inability to dissociate value from norm processing, limited lateralisation evidence, and compensatory reorganisation in lesion findings.

Key Findings

What the Synthesis Shows

Five principal conclusions emerging from the narrative synthesis, grounded in neuroimaging, computational modelling, and causal evidence.

01
AI Is Not a Fairness-Specific Processor

The anterior insula encodes unsigned prediction error magnitude — salience — regardless of valence. Its U-shaped response to norm violations (Xiang et al., 2013) and broad co-activation across task domains (Yarkoni et al., 2011) rule out a fairness-specific function. Activating AI alone carries high reverse inference risk.

Xiang et al., 2013 Menon & Uddin, 2010 Ruff & Fehr, 2014
02
VS Is the Strongest Point of Overlap

The ventral striatum encodes signed dopaminergic RPE that generalises across social and non-social domains. Its valence-specific gradient — positive norm PE ventromedially, negative norm PE dorsolaterally — mirrors the canonical PE architecture of non-social reward learning.

Xiang et al., 2013 Sescousse et al., 2013 Xu et al., 2025
03
vmPFC Integrates Value with Social Context

vmPFC transforms VS PE signals into socially-weighted subjective fairness value, sensitive to ingroup/partner-type context. Causal evidence from vmPFC lesions confirms impairment of other-directed but not self-directed reward learning — a functional specialisation within the shared PE network.

Gueguen et al., 2025 Apps et al., 2018 Christopoulos & King-Casas, 2015
04
dACC Monitors Conflict, Not RPE

dACC shows no consistent direct RPE encoding. Instead it tracks value differences between competing offers and scales with violation severity. It operates downstream of VS PE processing, coupling with the dorsolateral striatum during negative norm PE to guide behavioural adjustment.

Santo-Angles et al., 2021 Xu et al., 2025 Xiang et al., 2013
05
Fairness Is a Domain-General RL Computation

The synthesis supports a partial-overlap model: fairness processing recruits domain-general PE mechanisms rather than a dedicated module. Norm expectations are formed by the mentalising network, violations detected by AI, PEs computed in VS, values integrated in vmPFC, and conflict resolved by dACC — a coordinated, general-purpose architecture.

Schultz, 2016 Botvinick et al., 2009 Jin et al., 2025

Limitations & Caveats

Interpreting the Evidence

Several methodological constraints limit the conclusions that can be drawn from fMRI and correlational evidence in this synthesis.

🔁

Reverse Inference Risk

Activating the anterior insula during norm violation cannot be taken as evidence of fairness-specific processing. The AI is among the most broadly co-activated regions across task domains (Yarkoni et al., 2011), making reverse inference particularly hazardous without converging evidence.

📉

Activation ≠ Causation

Neuroimaging cannot distinguish reward responses from expectation updating or anticipatory processes (Pool et al., 2022). VS activation during norm enforcement may reflect PE, anticipation, or affective responses — fMRI alone cannot resolve this dissociation.

🧩

Dissociation Limits of fMRI

vmPFC value representations and VS PE signals are model-derived dissociations. fMRI cannot confirm causal directionality between PE encoding and value integration without TMS or lesion evidence.

🧠

Lesion Interpretation

Lesion findings (e.g., Gueguen et al., 2025) must be interpreted cautiously given variability in lesion characteristics, lesion overlap across participants, and compensatory neural reorganisation that may mask or exaggerate effects.

🔬

Lateralisation Evidence

Lateralisation of ventral striatal PE signals in the fairness context remains limited. Most studies do not report hemisphere-specific analyses, reducing confidence in claims about left vs. right striatal encoding of positive and negative norm PE.

👥

Ecological Validity

The widespread use of anonymous, unfamiliar partners in UG paradigms may underestimate vmPFC involvement in naturalistic fairness processing, where social relationship and reputation are salient (Apps et al., 2018; Brüne et al., 2013).
